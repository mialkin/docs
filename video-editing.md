# Video editing

## Rec. 709

**Rec. 709** is the universal industry standard for HD video. It acts as a "common language" between cameras and displays, establishing the exact color palette (gamut) and brightness curves (gamma) so your videos look consistent across TVs, phones, and monitors.

Professionals often shoot in flat, high-dynamic-range Log profiles to preserve more highlight and shadow data. They then use a Rec. 709 conversion (often via a LUT) to transform that flat footage into standard, viewable colors during the final editing stage.

## Log

A **Log** (logarithmic) **profile** is a camera setting that captures a wider dynamic range, preserving maximum detail in the brightest and darkest areas of an image.

Footage shot in Log looks incredibly flat and desaturated out of the camera, requiring color grading in post-production to achieve a normal, cinematic appearance.

## LUT

**LUT** (Look-Up Table) is most commonly used in video editing and photography as a color-grading preset. It acts as a mathematical formula that instantly transforms the color and contrast of an image or video to achieve a specific visual style.

## Powergrade

A **PowerGrade** within DaVinci Resolve is a custom color-grading preset that saves an entire node tree structure and all of its underlying adjustments.

Unlike a standard project asset, a PowerGrade is saved globally within your DaVinci Resolve database. This means it is instantly accessible across all timelines and completely different projects on your system.
